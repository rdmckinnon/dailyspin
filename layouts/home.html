{{/* ============================================ HERO: THE MANIFESTO ============================================ */}}

SOMETHING BROKE.

Somewhere between Spotify's algorithm and TikTok's 15-second attention span, we stopped listening to music. We started consuming content.

The science is simple now. Repeat the hook every 30 seconds. Drop the chorus before anyone gets bored. Engineer the dopamine hit. Test it. Tweak it. Ship it.

It works. The numbers prove it.

But here's the thing—you can engineer a craving without ever satisfying it. You can write a song that gets stuck in someone's head without ever touching their heart.

Remember when songs were built to last?

When a band spent three years in a basement getting the guitar tone right. When a songwriter rewrote the bridge sixteen times because almost wasn't good enough. When a song could wreck you—not because it was loud, but because it was true.

That's what we're chasing here.

One song. One story. Every day.

Not the algorithm's pick. Not what's trending.
Just music that still believes in the slow build, the earned crescendo, the silence before the thing that changes everything.
